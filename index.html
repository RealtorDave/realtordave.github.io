Leukemia

What is Leukemia and What Causes It

Leukemia is the cancer of the blood and bone marrow. It is the rapid growth of abnormal cells in the bone marrow, the blood making tissue. It suppresses the production of normal blood cells, such as red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets. It usually results in an extremely elevated count of defective white blood cells. Normally, white blood cells account for 1% of your blood volume (around 4000-10000 cells) and are meant to fight infection. Not only are leukemia cells bad at fighting infection, they hurt the ability of red blood cells and platelets to do their jobs. This happens because leukemia causes an overproduction of blast cells. Blast cells are basically the stem cells of blood. These blast cells don't properly develop and don't mature into blood cells. These blast cells eventually outnumber normal blood cells so that they cannot do their jobs. Unlike other cancers, leukemia doesn’t form a tumor that can be found by x-rays. Leukemia starts when the DNA of a single cell in the bone marrow changes (mutation) and can’t continue to function normally. It is currently unknown what causes the initial DNA mutation, however, there are some theories. Most studies show that leukemia is hereditary. It is also said that Artificial Ionising Radiation (radiation from xray tubes and particle accelerators) can cause leukemia.

Symptoms of Leukemia

Here are the main symptoms of leukemia:

  • Bone/Joint Pain
  • Easy Bleeding/Bruising
  • Unexplained Weight Loss
  • Persisent Fatigue/Weakness
  • Tiny Red Spots on Skin
  • Frequent Infections

What Systems does Leukemia Affect

Leukemia affects multiple systems directly and indirectly. Here is an explanation of what it does to each system.

Circulatory System

Since leukemia is blood cancer, leukemia directly affects the circulatory system. Here are a few ways:
⚫Leukemia subdues the creation of normal blood cells
⚫Increased production of defective white blood cells
⚫Defective cells vastly outnumber good cells and hinder their ability to properly function
⚫Due to the increase in white blood cells, blood vessels can get clogged
⚫If there are too many cancer cells in the bone marrow, the bone marrow can clog up, which causes no blood to be released into blood vessels at all. This creates further problems like anemia.

Skeletal System

Leukemia both directly and indirectly affects the skeletal system
⚫Leukemia causes joint and bone pain
⚫This is because bone marrow becomes overcrowded with cancer cells. These cancer cells can form masses near joints and the nerves of the spinal cord, causing pain
⚫Leukemia also caused bone thinning, similar to osteoporosis

Digestive System

Leukemia does not directly have an affect on the digestive system, however several factors that affect other systems translate into issues with the digestive system. Here's a list:
⚫Due to a lack of platelets, your gums can bleed easily. This leads to mouth discomfort and makes it very hard to eat
⚫Leukemia cells can gather in your liver, causing bloating. This gives you a sense of "fullness", which makes it hard to eat
⚫Treatments for leukemia, like chemotherapy and radiation, cause nausea and vomiting. This leads to a lack of appetite
